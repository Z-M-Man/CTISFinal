Second-Rate

Second-rate ships were powerful ships that could be considered lighter and cheaper versions of first-rates. A typical crew size was about 700-750 members. They carried 80-100 guns of lighter caliber than first-rates: 32-pounders on the lowest gun deck, 18-pounders on the middle deck, and 12-pounders on the third deck. Lighter guns like carronades were still carried on the topmost decks. They were still considered powerful enough to participate in a line of battle, but they were also used as flagships in overseas colonies--a task considered too risky for first-rates. Being shorter than first-rates while having the same number of desks, they became notorious for being slow and cumbersome; a problem so prevalent that by 1814, only seven second-rates were still in commission. In the final decades of sail after the Napoleonic Wars, they were downsized to have only two decks.
