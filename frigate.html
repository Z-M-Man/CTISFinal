The Frigate

History

The frigate type of ship can be traced to a galley-type warship, driven by oars and lightly armed. In 1853, privateers known as the "Dunkirkers" developed small, maneuverable ships to attack Dutch shipping. These were the first sailing ships to be referred to as "frigates." Soon, the Dutch themselves built larger frigates that were able to travel oceans. These ships were used with great success, and soon other European navies started to adopt similar designs. The French developed a frigate design with one cannon deck and a lower hull, giving it greatly improved agility. The British captured some of these French ships and based many of their own frigates on those.

Characteristics

While early frigates varied in design, the French "true frigate" is the most well-known and widely used during the Napoleonic Wars. The frigate's fast speed made it both a great escort against privateer ships and a formidable shipping raider itself. In a battle between ship of the lines, frigates would avoid direct combat with any larger ship; instead, they served as scouts and relayed messages from the flagship to the rest of the fleet. Smaller fourth-rate ships were sometimes informally referred to as "frigates."
