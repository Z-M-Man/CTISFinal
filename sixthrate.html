Sixth-Rate

Sixth-rates were older, smaller frigates and the smallest warship classified under the British "Rating" system. They only carried 20-24 cannons and a crew of 140-160. Sixth-rates in the early 18th century were equipped with 6-pounders, but 9-pounders soon replaced them mid-century. As with fifth-rates, they were assigned to escorting convoys and overseas stations. By the time of the Napoleonic Wars, they were too small to carry out frigate duties expected of them and mostly phased out. About 25 of them lasted to 1814, still serving in auxiliary roles like escorting.
